About

Louis Guichard is a French photographer and visual artist based between Europe and the United States.

After five years working in audiovisual production, film and video in Paris, he left for New York and spent the following years travelling across the United States, China and India.

Working exclusively with 35mm film, his photographs remain entirely unretouched, preserving the integrity of the original negative and the spontaneity of the moment captured.

His work explores the space between reality and perception. Rather than documenting places with precision, he is interested in what they leave behind: a feeling, a memory, a trace.

For Louis, a photograph is not an answer but an invitation, a space where the viewer is free to bring their own memories, emotions and interpretations.

In a time when everything must be immediate, explained and consumed at once, he remains attached to the idea that a photograph can still resist; that it can preserve mystery, leave room for interpretation, and give value to what remains unsaid.
